Problem Statement

Known

A free water jet enters a smooth stationary vane and leaves with the same speed but with a changed direction.

Atmospheric pressure surrounds the jet, and weight and viscous effects are neglected.

Find

Determine the force components needed to hold the vane fixed as the flow is redirected.

Analysis

With a fixed control volume around the vane, the steady linear momentum equation reduces to a flux balance between the inlet and outlet jets.

\[ \sum F_x = \dot{m}(V_{2x}-V_{1x}),\qquad \sum F_z = \dot{m}(V_{2z}-V_{1z}) \]

The inlet velocity is horizontal. If the exit direction is turned upward by \(\theta\),

\[ V_{1x}=V,\quad V_{1z}=0,\quad V_{2x}=V\cos\theta,\quad V_{2z}=V\sin\theta \]

Using \(\dot{m}=\rho A V\), the holding-force components become

\[ F_x=-\rho A V^2(1-\cos\theta),\qquad F_z=\rho A V^2\sin\theta \]
